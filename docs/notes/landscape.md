# The adult math-learning discourse — the camps

- **Mastery / deliberate practice** — Math Academy (+ *The Math Academy Way*, Skycak), Oakley's *Learning How to Learn*, Brilliant, Khan as baseline.
- **Visual intuition** — 3Blue1Brown, Better Explained, Strogatz's trade books.
- **Depth / first-principles self-study** — Oz Nova / csprimer, Scott Young (*Ultralearning*, MIT Challenge), "read the real textbook."
- **Programming-first math** — Jeremy Kun, *A Programmer's Introduction to Mathematics*.
- **Beauty / why math matters** — Lockhart's *Lament*, Francis Su's *Mathematics for Human Flourishing*, Hardy, the Hungarian/olympiad tradition.
- **Learning-science tooling** — Nielsen & Matuschak: spaced repetition aimed at understanding, not recall.

## My takes
- **3Blue1Brown / Brilliant** — cultural-tourism risk: watch, feel something, retain little; no reps, dropped in without prerequisites. The critique is really about *passive watching*, not the videos — Sanderson says the same (you retain little from watching; you have to do problems).
- **Strogatz** — two registers: the trade books (*Joy of x*, *Infinite Powers*) are genuinely beginner-accessible; *Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos* is a hard, topic-specific textbook. My "niche" impression was the textbook side.
- **Oakley, *Learning How to Learn*** — good; absorbed.
- **Spaced repetition** — more than scaffolding when aimed at understanding (see resources). Not the whole game; I want intuition first.
- **Math Academy** — gym / supplement, not the driver.
- **Scott Young / MIT Challenge** — passing 33 MIT CS courses in 12 months; reads as performative. A data point on aggressive self-study, not a model.

## The real axis (the frustration)
Two virtues rarely show up together:
- **(a) beauty / intuition / exposition**, and
- **(b) exercises + prerequisite structure.**

The popular-math camp (Princeton Companion, Su, Strogatz trade books, 3b1b) is (a) without (b): exposition, no exercises, topics dropped in without prerequisites. Math Academy is (b) without (a): real prerequisite graph + practice + spaced review, but narrow and gamified. Almost nothing has both. The frustration is accurate, not a me-problem.

Prerequisite-structure and spaced repetition are different layers. Math Academy *fuses* them (knowledge graph + spaced review over it). Nielsen/Matuschak SRS is only the retention/understanding layer — no prerequisites; you supply the sequencing. Building the curriculum/reader by hand *is* the prerequisite work — and that structuring is itself deep processing, much of what SRS is trying to manufacture.
